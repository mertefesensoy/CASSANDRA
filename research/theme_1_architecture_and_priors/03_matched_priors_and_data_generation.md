# 11. Matched Priors and the Data Generating Function

**Relevant Cassandra Stages:** Stage 26 and Stage 27

## Exhaustive Methodology Review

A fundamental principle of statistical learning and Bayesian inference is that the efficacy of a prior depends entirely on how well it maps to the true distribution of the world. In neural networks, this is often discussed in terms of **Inductive Bias Alignment**.

### Inductive Bias Alignment
Research demonstrates that a model achieves maximum sample efficiency when its architectural constraints perfectly align with the inherent structure of the task. 
1. **The Matched Prior:** If the true data generating process is a graph with a specific locality radius, a Graph Neural Network with a matching aggregation depth will learn almost instantly. If the data is generated by an $n$-th order Markov process, a model initialized with an exact $n$-gram prior will have a perfectly flat loss curve; it already "knows" the answer.
2. **The Misspecified Prior:** A prior is misspecified if it deviates from the true population distribution. 
    - **Underspecified:** The prior lacks the complexity to model the data (e.g., assuming an order-1 dependency when the data requires order-2). The model will underfit unless it has a powerful, dense neural path that can overcome the rigid prior.
    - **Overspecified:** The prior assumes complex dependencies that do not exist in the data (e.g., using a deep trigram prior for purely order-1 data). This can lead to overfitting on noise or wasting parameter capacity, eventually losing to a simpler or more general model that correctly discovers the true sparsity.

### Misspecification vs. Scaling Compute
When a prior is perfectly matched, "The Bitter Lesson" (where general compute overtakes human heuristics) is infinitely delayed. The general neural network must spend thousands of iterations slowly converging on the exact probability distribution that the matched prior already possesses at step zero. However, when the prior is misspecified, the general neural network rapidly discovers the true data generating function from the raw data, exposing the prior as a bottleneck rather than a shortcut.

## Relevance to Cassandra's Tiny Language Lab

**Direct Link to Staged Experiments:**
Cassandra explicitly demonstrated Inductive Bias Alignment in **Stage 26** and
completed the first local surface in **Stage 27**.
- **The Setup:** Codex generated a pure order-2 Markov corpus (trigram
  transitions) and an order-1 Markov corpus (bigram transitions). It then tested
  an order-1 prior (`count_prior_lora_r2`) and an order-2 prior
  (`count_prior_tri_lora_r2`) against full neural training.
- **The Matched Prior Win:** On the order-2 data, the matched trigram prior stayed
  strongly ahead of full training through 200 steps. On the order-1 data, the
  matched bigram prior also stayed positive through 200 steps.
- **The Misspecified Penalty:** On the order-2 data, the underspecified bigram
  prior lost its advantage entirely, tying with full training by 200 steps. On
  the order-1 data, the overspecified trigram prior became negative by 100 and
  200 steps.
